Serve ball & past-set ladder

Revision 2, updated after your comments: real BV550C photo cutout as the serve ball, past sets directly under the top bar, current-set label centred between the points. Mocked with the board's real spacing, type and container-query sizing (BroadcastBoard.tsx). Nothing is implemented yet — comment on anything and I'll revise before touching code.

What changes

  1. Serve indicator ball — the real BV550C

    The exact circular cutout of the ball photo you provided (background removed, re-rounded to a true circle), now small — a quarter of the previous size — sitting on the points line between the "Set N" label and the serving team's score. It switches sides on side-out and is static (no float — a dot this size reads better still). Nothing else moves when it flips. The board already receives the serving prop — it's currently unused, so no data work is needed.

  2. Past sets just below the top bar

    Completed sets stack vertically right under the framed team-names bar — "Set 1  21:13" / "Set 2  13:21" — with the losing score dimmed, matching the dimming rule the old ladder used. The current set is never listed; before Set 2 the block simply isn't there.

  3. Current-set label centred between the points

    "Set N" moves to precisely the middle of the board, vertically centred between the two big numbers — taking the spot of the old boxed set-ladder, which goes away (its information now lives in the past-set stack and the sets-won boxes in the top bar). The label is bare text: the two accent squares that used to flank it are removed on all boards.

  4. Animations

    Two motions, both live on screen 1 below (use the buttons): on every point the score rolls like an odometer — the new number drops in from above while the old one falls away and fades. On a side-out only, the ball leaves its spot, flies a V — bouncing at the bottom centre, above the logo area — and comes back up beside the label on the other side (~0.9 s, with a half-spin down and a half-spin up). The production board will skip both for reduced-motion viewers; this demo always plays them (use the slow-motion toggle to study the moves).

Open decisions

  • Ball asset: the cutout ships as a static image in public/. It shows the ball's rear panel with the "FIVB APPROVED" print — at board size the text reads as texture. If you'd rather have the logo-free front face, send that photo and I'll cut it the same way.
  • Ball size: now 6 cqmin (doubled per your note) — slightly taller than the "Set N" text. Can still go up or down.
  • Ball offset: anchored 6 cqmin off the label's edge, toward the serving side. It can instead sit exactly midway to the score digit if you prefer.
